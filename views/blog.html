What are the best cat treats?

Date: 04/01/2023

Subject: cat care

Author: Keeley

Post:

Cats are beloved members of many families, and one of the ways to show them love and affection is through treats. However, not all treats are created equal. Some treats may be unhealthy, while others may not be well-suited to a cat's dietary needs. In this blog post, we'll discuss some of the best treats to give your feline friend.

  1. Fresh fruits and vegetables. Cats are obligate carnivores, which means that their diet should be primarily made up of meat. However, small amounts of fresh fruits and vegetables can be a healthy addition to their diet. Some great options include diced apple, blueberries, and cooked sweet potatoes.
  2. Cooked, plain chicken. Chicken is a great source of protein for cats, and cooking it yourself ensures that it is free from additives and preservatives. You can offer it as small slices or shreds as a treat.
  3. Fish. Cats love fish, and it's a great source of omega-3 fatty acids, which are beneficial for their skin and coat. Offer small pieces of cooked, plain fish, such as salmon or tuna.
  4. Store-bought cat treats. If you prefer to buy treats, look for ones that are made with natural, high-quality ingredients. Avoid treats that are high in sugar or contain artificial preservatives.
  5. Homemade cat treats. Another option is to make your own cat treats at home. Some easy recipes include mixing together cooked, mashed sweet potato and egg or grinding up cooked chicken or fish and forming the mixture into small meatballs.

It's important to note that treats should be given in moderation, as they are not intended to replace a cat's regular diet. With a variety of healthy and delicious options, you can spoil your cat without compromising their health.

Always remember to check with your Vet before introducing any new food item to your cat diet, some cats may have food allergies or other health condition that may not make them suitable for certain food item.
